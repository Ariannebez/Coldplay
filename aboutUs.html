Coldplay Band


British rock group Coldplay was founded in 1997 in London. Chris Martin, who sings and plays the piano, Jonny Buckland, Guy Berryman, Will Champion, and Phil Harvey, the group's creative director, round out the lineup.From 1996 to 1998, they collaborated on music under the name Starfish after meeting at University College London.


Coldplay joined Parlophone in 1999 after independently releasing an extended play called Safety in 1998. The group's debut album, Parachutes (2000), featured their breakout single "Yellow," for which they were nominated for a Grammy Award for Best Alternative Music Album, a Brit Award for British Album of the Year, and a Mercury Prize. The track "Clocks," which earned the Grammy Award for Record of the Year, was featured on their second album, A Rush of Blood to the Head (2002), which received the same accolades.
