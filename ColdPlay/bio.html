Chris Martin

Christopher Anthony John Martin is an English singer-songwriter and musician who was born on March 2, 1977. He is most known for being the co-founder, lead vocalist, keyboardist, and rhythm guitarist of the rock band Coldplay. He attended University College London after attending Exeter, Devon, where he met other students Jonny Buckland, Guy Berryman, and Will Champion to establish the band. With the release of the song "Yellow" in 2000, they gained recognition on a global scale and went on to get praise for albums like "A Rush of Blood to the Head" (2002), "Viva la Vida or Death and All His Friends" (2008), among others. As a member of the band, he received nine Brit Awards and seven Grammy Awards. As of 2021, they had sold more than 100 million records globally, making them the most popular.

Guy Berryman

Guy Rupert Berryman, a Scottish musician, composer, and producer, was born on April 12, 1978. He is best known as the bassist for Coldplay and the electronic supergroup Apparatjik. He was raised in Kirkcaldy and picked up the bass guitar at a young age, taking inspiration from artists like James Brown, Kool & the Gang, and The Funk Brothers. He established the fashion brand Applied Art Forms in 2020, serving as its creative director and designer. Berryman joined Coldplay at University College London alongside Chris Martin, Jonny Buckland, and Will Champion after enrolling there to study mechanical engineering but quitting.

Jonny Buckland

Jonathan Mark Buckland, a Welsh musician and songwriter who was born in England on September 11, 1977, is best known as the lead guitarist and co-founder of the rock group Coldplay. He was raised in Pantymwyn and picked up the guitar at a young age, listening to bands like The Stone Roses, My Bloody Valentine, and U2. His use of the slide bar and delay pedal with a distinctive chiming and ringing timbre has led to comparisons with the Edge. He is known for his sparse and delicate arrangements.

Will Champion

William Champion, an English musician and composer born on July 31, 1978, is best known for his work as Coldplay's drummer and backup singer. Growing up in Southampton, he learned how to play a variety of instruments while being influenced by traditional Irish folk, Bob Dylan, Tom Waits, and Nick Cave. He prioritizes the key components of a song in his dynamic drumming technique, and he is occasionally known to assume the lead vocals during live performances.
